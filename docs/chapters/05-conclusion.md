## Chapter 6: Conclusion

The present study addressed a genuine and consequential gap in the empirical literature on nursing practice in Slovakia by conducting the first systematic quantitative survey of digital technology use, technology attitudes, and perceived quality impact among clinical nurses in Slovak inpatient and outpatient healthcare settings. The study's contributions to the scholarly literature are threefold. First, it provides the first nationally grounded descriptive data on digital tool use frequency, tool type distribution, and EHR quality perceptions in Slovak clinical nursing — data that can serve as a baseline against which future surveys can measure change as the eZdravie infrastructure matures and as digital competency requirements evolve under EU Health Data Space regulation. Second, it applies and partially tests the theoretical predictions of TAM, UTAUT, and Diffusion of Innovations theory in a Slovak nursing population, contributing to the international evidence base for these frameworks in a Central European context that has been underrepresented in the published literature. Third, it identifies concrete, evidence-based directions for intervention at the levels of nursing education, continuing professional development, healthcare facility IT governance, and national health informatics policy.

The limitations of the study — particularly the convenience sampling strategy, the cross-sectional design, and the non-standardised instrument — constrain the generalisability of findings and preclude causal inference. Future research in this domain should employ probability sampling across multiple Slovak regions, a validated and psychometrically characterised questionnaire instrument adapted for the Slovak nursing context, and a longitudinal follow-up design capable of tracking changes in digital adoption, attitudes, and quality perceptions as eZdravie implementation matures across healthcare facilities. Mixed-methods designs incorporating qualitative interview data would enrich understanding of the mechanisms underlying the quantitative associations observed in this study, particularly the contextual conditions under which the experience-based attitudinal gradient is attenuated or reversed by institutional support.

Digital transformation of healthcare is not a background trend but the immediate structural reality in which Slovak nurses practice. The adequacy of that practice — its safety, its efficiency, and its quality — will depend increasingly on nurses' capacity to engage competently and confidently with the digital tools that mediate their clinical work. This study is offered as an empirical contribution to the evidence base on which that capacity can be systematically built.
