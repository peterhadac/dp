## Theoretical Background

### Theory 1: Sociotechnical Systems Theory (Leavitt, 1965; adapted to digital health)

**Originator:** Harold Leavitt (1965); adapted to eHealth contexts by Sittig and Singh (2010) and operationalized by Abdolkhani et al. (2022) using an 8-dimension sociotechnical framework.

**Core content:** Organizations are composed of four interdependent elements — people, tasks, technology, and structure — that must be balanced for successful change. In digital health, the model expands to examine hardware/software, clinical content, human factors, workflow, policies, culture, and system measurement.

**Relevance to this study:** Telemedicine implementation in nursing cannot be reduced to technology alone. Abdolkhani et al. (2022) explicitly applied this framework to nurses during COVID-19 and found that "people" and "workflow" dimensions were the most disrupted and the least systematically addressed, providing a theoretical lens for understanding adoption barriers and competency gaps.

---

### Theory 2: Diffusion of Innovations Theory (Rogers, 1962/2003)

**Originator:** Everett M. Rogers (Diffusion of Innovations, 1962; 5th ed. 2003)

**Core content:** Innovations spread through a social system via five adopter categories (innovators, early adopters, early majority, late majority, laggards) and are influenced by five perceived attributes: relative advantage, compatibility, complexity, trialability, and observability.

**Relevance to this study:** Post-COVID acceleration of telemedicine represents a forced diffusion event. Martin et al. (2024) and Bártlová et al. (2023) both demonstrate that after an initial adoption peak during the pandemic, diffusion stalled in some settings once restrictions lifted — consistent with Rogers's "compatibility" and "trialability" barriers. Only 4.9% of Czech nurses actively used telenursing systems post-COVID (Bártlová et al., 2023), reflecting late-majority or laggard positioning.

---

### Theory 3: Competency-Based Education and Practice Framework (ANA Nursing Informatics Competencies)

**Originator:** American Nurses Association (ANA, 2015, revised 2022); operationalized in telehealth contexts by Fisk et al. (2021) and Tischendorf et al. (2024)

**Core content:** Nursing competency frameworks define the knowledge, skills, and attitudes (KSA) required for professional practice. The 2022 Nurse Practitioner Role Core Competencies introduced Domain 8: Technology and Information Literacy, explicitly incorporating telehealth systems.

**Relevance to this study:** This framework anchors the section on required nurse competencies for telemedicine, providing a structured taxonomy against which current deficits (identified by Livesay et al., 2023, and Tischendorf et al., 2024) can be measured.

## Research Gap Summary

1. **Absence of Slovak-specific empirical data on telemedicine nursing adoption:** While Czech data from Bártlová et al. (2023) and Angelovská et al. (2024) provide the closest approximation, no study has directly surveyed Slovak nursing staff on telemedicine adoption rates, attitudes, or competency self-assessment. This is the primary gap this thesis addresses.

2. **No validated European nursing digital competency curriculum:** Tischendorf et al. (2024) confirm that a scoping review of PubMed and CINAHL (2017–2024) found no purpose-built continuing education program for nursing digital competencies in the European context. Slovak nursing education reform lacks an evidence-based competency map.

3. **Regulatory-implementation gap in Slovakia:** The 2024 Slovak Health Care Act amendment legally permits telemedicine nursing but provides no reimbursement mechanism, no telemedicine-specific standards of care, and no professional guidelines. No published study has examined how Slovak nurses experience this regulatory ambiguity in practice.

4. **Post-pandemic sustainability gap in Central Europe:** Existing sustainability research (Martin et al., 2024; Abdolkhani et al., 2022) is conducted in Western European or Anglo-Saxon contexts. The specific organizational, political, and cultural factors affecting post-COVID telemedicine sustainability in Slovak and Czech healthcare systems are unstudied.

5. **Outcomes evidence gap for Slovak patient populations:** All major outcome studies (Lo Monaco et al., 2025; Kemp Van Ee et al., 2022; Bruce et al., 2024) originate outside Central Europe. Whether outcome benefits replicate in Slovak healthcare — with its distinct healthcare financing model, infrastructure, and demographic profile — is unknown.

## Proposed Theoretical Framework

This thesis proposes integrating three theoretical traditions into a composite framework:

**The Sociotechnical Telemedicine Nursing Integration Model (ST-TNIM)**

The framework posits that telemedicine integration into Slovak nursing practice is determined by the dynamic interaction of three layers:

- **Macro layer (Regulatory/Policy):** Legislative framework (2024 Slovak amendment), reimbursement policy, professional standards. Based on institutional theory and the Czech/Slovak legal analysis.

- **Meso layer (Organizational):** Leadership commitment, workflow redesign, organizational culture, training infrastructure. Based on Diffusion of Innovations Theory (Rogers) and the 8-dimension sociotechnical model (Abdolkhani et al., 2022).

- **Micro layer (Individual Nurse):** Digital competency, attitudes toward telemedicine, self-efficacy, professional identity. Based on the competency framework of Al Baalharith & Aboshaiqah (2024) and Tischendorf et al. (2024).

Patient outcomes (safety, satisfaction, clinical improvement) are the dependent variable. The framework predicts that telemedicine adoption and effectiveness are impossible if any one layer is absent — regulatory authorization without organizational support and nurse competency produces the current Slovak situation: legal permission but de facto non-implementation.

## Handoff to Methodology Expert

The literature review identifies the following methodological implications for the empirical phase of this thesis:

- **Recommended design:** Cross-sectional descriptive survey of Slovak registered nurses, with a comparative element referencing Czech data (Bártlová et al., 2023) as a benchmark.
- **Instrument:** A validated or adapted questionnaire covering telemedicine adoption rates, attitude scales, self-assessed competency inventory aligned with the 12-domain framework (Al Baalharith & Aboshaiqah, 2024), and perceived barriers taxonomy derived from Nabelsi et al. (2025) and Chua et al. (2024).
- **Methodology trends from prior studies:** Most prior studies in Czech/Slovak context use non-standardized sociological questionnaires with face-to-face administration (Bártlová et al., 2023); international studies increasingly use validated digital instruments. A hybrid approach is recommended.
